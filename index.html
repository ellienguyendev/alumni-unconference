WHY AN UNCONFERENCE?

Intentions for Holding the Event

A Message from the Staff

Hi Alumni, Staff has been meeting over the past couple of weeks to discuss Leon’s message on the alumni channel, Del’s follow up comments, and the conversations that unfolded in the aftermath. We see you and we hear you. Each one of us is reflecting on our actions and we’re in the process of rethinking our relationship with the alumni community. Del has said it many times before, You Are The Movement, and we want to make sure we’re collectively working together, especially as we expand to other cities and as our community grows.

Many of you expressed interest in participating in a town hall type of event, and many of you were also kind enough to meet with us one-on-one to express how you felt and your feedback. We know that wasn’t easy, and we are grateful for your time and energy. In an effort to also be more transparent and build accountability, we’d love to come together to listen and share in our first-ever Resilient Coders Alumni Unconference.

We’ll be holding an “open floor” session, hosting break-out sessions, and ending with a community celebration. There are three main areas that we’ve identified:

  • 1. Community Accountability.

    a. Reviewing, re-creating and reaffirming our mission and values

    b. Addressing harm within our own community

    c. Brave spaces: how to build a community in which we support and challenge one another?

    d. Transforming and Re-imagining

  • 2. Transparency on Resilient Coders’ Operations. Staff will make themselves available to discuss various aspects of our operations including recruitment, fundraising, expansion plans, employer partnerships, curriculum, and the bootcamp experience.

  • 3. Alumni Voice and Organizing. You are the movement. This is your organization and this is your community. We’d like to open up space to discuss what alumni engagement should look like at RC.

Community Guidelines

Created together on Day 1

  • Don’t engage from a place of: shame, insult, guilt, and the need to be right
  • Use I statements
  • Listen with intention. Listen to understand, don’t listen to respond
  • Being valued
  • Be open minded
  • Be intentional
  • Don’t dismiss another’s ideas before asking for clarification.
  • Just because you haven’t experienced something yourself, doesn’t mean that experience isn’t valid
  • No tone policing, and conversely, speak up with care
  • Step up, step back. Encourage folks who may not usually speak
  • Don’t need to be an expert to share
  • Avoid ableist language
  • Avoid derogatory terms
  • Lean into discomfort
  • Checking our privilege
  • Being slow to respond, taking a moment to answer especially if there's initial heated emotions
  • Don’t assume intentions. Name impact.
  • One mic. One voice. Don’t interrupt!
  • If speaking about situations, avoid blame or pointing fingers
    "you did this you made me feel this, etc." and talk about how --I--- felt or how it impacted --me--
  • Respect confidentiality

F.A.Q

  • Where is this event happening?

    Kick-off will be on Remo, then Google Meets. Stephanie will send calendar invites. Check back for Day 2 schedule for links to breakout sessions. If you are unable to get in or did not receive a calendar invite - please message a member of Staff.

  • Can I share this with folks outside of RC?

    For confidentiality and trust, please keep this within the alumni community.

  • Is this going to be a yearly event?

    Not necessarily, no. We hope that this will a be an ongoing conversation and effort so we will plan more events in collaboration with the alumni community.

  • Where can I give feedback?

    Please provide feedback for the event using this survey form or message a member of Staff (Del, Leon, Stephanie, Rougui, Vonds, Flex, Lexi, Atiya, Ellie) for other comments or concerns you may have.
